Carddeck 'Bromvliegeffect'

assignment

During my Behavior Design minor, I delved into the psychology behind the 'bromvliegeffect' and created a card deck to help aspiring UX designers. By exploring cognitive biases and heuristics offering practical tips for designers. To ensure a comprehensive understanding. It includes 12 cards on psychological principles and 6 on the ethical considerations designers should keep in mind.

process

My first two week in this behavior design course opened my eyes to a world of possibilities. I decided to focus on the 'bromvliegeffect' for my card deck because I think it has a lot of potential for UX designers. The 'bromvliegeffect' refers to the phenomenon where a seemingly small and insignificant annoyance can have a disproportionately large impact on our behavior or experience. Just like a fly buzzing around your head, it can be a constant distraction that ruins your focus or mood. I created 12 cards exploring different aspects of the 'bromvliegeffect', including related biases and heuristics and practical design tips. I also included 6 cards on the ethical implications of using these insights in UX design.
